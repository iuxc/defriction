2. The Current State: "The PDF Trap" (This is the new storytelling section you asked for. It sets up the villain.)

Headline: The Problem: Admissions by Administrative Burden.

Narrative: "Currently, applying to university isn't just about grades; it's about navigating a maze. Students are forced to self-diagnose their eligibility using static PDFs, hidden 'Adjustment Factor' spreadsheets, and confusing academic acronyms (VTAC, SEAS, DoTS). The system acts as a Gatekeeper, checking IDs at the door, rather than a Concierge helping guests find their way."

Friction Points (The Evidence):

The Dead End: A TAFE graduate searching for credit gets 0 results because the database is incomplete.

The Hidden Math: A regional student with an ATAR of 65 sees a cutoff of 70 and abandons the site, unaware that their postcode grants them +5 points.

The "Double Handling": Applicants are bounced to external sites (VTAC) to pay $200 fees for courses they could have applied to directly for free.

Headline: The Shift to Equity.

Narrative: "The 2024 Universities Accord mandates a shift from a market-driven logic to an equity-driven mandate. 4. The Solution: "The Pathways Finder"

Headline: Visualizing the Invisible.

Narrative: "We designed a dynamic component that visualizes the 'Hidden Math' of admissions. Instead of a static table, the 'Stacking Calculator' shows students exactly how close they are."
